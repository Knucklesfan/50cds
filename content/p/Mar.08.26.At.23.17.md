I'm not a huge fan of King Gizzard's other works before 2024. It's a weird thing to say, and usually when I say something like that I hear "Really? But their stuff *BEFORE* Flight b471 is so much better!" and I know. I get the appeal, but to me it's way too... metal.

I don't really like heavy rock. Personally, when the guitar starts getting overdriven I get nervous, and if that overdrive stays throughout the majority of the track and it's *too* loud, I'll usually avoid the track. Yes, I know this is a dumb take. Yes, I'm aware that most people feel the direct opposite and entire genres of music exist directly due to overdriving the guitar.

That's exactly why Phantom Island is exactly my kind of groove. It still lingers a little bit of their heavy rock history, but god that psychedelic/jazz/classical combination in this album is incredible.

Nothing, and I mean nothing, will match the high of cranking the car, driving out of the neighborhood and hitting the highway RIGHT when Phantom Island his at 3:32. It's a **RUSH**. It's pure, unadulterated SPEED. A spiral downward, an escape to the phantom island. Full, pure escapism at it's best. Phantom Island, Insane Asylum.

Most of the tracks on this album are fantastic, and come from a deeply personal place for the authors. Take Lonely Cosmos, Panpsych, Spacesick, or Aerodynamic and you find a tale of sad loneliness. Loneliness and the escape from the day-to-day problems that we face is an overarching and never-ending theme throughout the entire album.

In no place is this more obvious than in my favorite track on the entire album, Eternal Return. It's a story of aging times, yet still a devotion and dedication to continue going onwards. It hurts so much because you can feel the pain of abandoning one's family for the dedication to continue one's artistic endeavours. The continued cold shoulder, the foolish decision to choose to leave your family for living your dream and touring. It's so painful to hear, the tug of war between the two just leaves the artist in the middle left torn apart. I think my favorite lyric in the entire track is this:

Which way is the right way down the yellow brick road of life
I'm tossing and turning between my time with a scarecrow and my tin man wife

I'll leave it at that. I think that describing this story any further just reveals my own pain in having to chose between doing what I love and leaving the ones that I love to do that thing, so these lyrics specifically speak to that side of me.

I don't want to ruin the rest of the album for you if you haven't listened to it yet. Just take my word for it and know you're in for a ride. It's a fantastic album and one that I think that everyone should give a try at least once.

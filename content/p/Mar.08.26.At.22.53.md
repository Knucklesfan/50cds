You know what sucks? Supertramp. You know what I love? Supertramp.

Probably the biggest duality in my life is whether or not I actually enjoy Supertramp, or think that it's hot balls. On one hand, you have tracks that have [the world's most surface level lyrics combined with great instrumentals (featuring hank jennings!)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vS7u8Ku_Nso), or you have [great vocal performances with meh instrumentals](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4yc6Qq3Hz70). Or god forbid you're [**potter**](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u20QNICdSq8) (i have no idea what to even say about this entire album, not to mention this song...)

To be honest, there are times where I'm somewhat embarassed to say that I enjoy Supertramp. I know that Supertramp is a good band! They've made genuinely great music! No closer can we get to all of my problems with Supertramp than to look at their 1975 album "Crisis? What Crisis?" because... It's so divisive for me.

Okay, so get this. Take an ambiently beautiful and easy entry song like Easy Does It, follow that into Sister Moonshine (a great track in it's own right), but then combine that with headscratchers like "A Soapbox Opera" and "The Meaning" with great instrumentals but god awful vocal performances, and you start to get confused. Each consecutive track into this album improves instrumentally and degrades vocally.

I mean, "The Meaning" sounds incredible! Absolutely breath-taking instrumental performance. But the vocals and the lyrics? I can't stand them. I don't know what the meaning is Rick, you don't need to keep asking me if I know what it is. 

Then you have the direct opposite side of the coin, Lady's instrumental is very meh to me, especially at the start, but the vocal performance combined with the later "Ooh Lalalala" section cleans the track up. 

When I was younger, I used to think that Rick Davies was the stuff of legends. I mean, look at his work on Breakfast in America and you'd realize right away what I mean. He seriously knew how to write a [compelling hooking track](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hbqVPYZgK48)! But as I get older, I start to realize that Roger Hodgeson really held something special in producing tracks that age well, and more importantly **survive as an album keeps going on**. I think that Davies really struggles with that "later album tracks" issue, to which I ask: HOW??? HOW CAN THE TRACKS GET WORSE AS THE ALBUM KEEPS GOING??? YOU MADE ALL OF THEM AT THE SAME TIME?????? 

I get it. They put their worse work towards the end. But personally, I could live with an album that didn't include tracks like "A Soapbox Opera" if it meant more tracks like "Poor Boy" or "Ain't Nobody but Me"

But really, I don't think I'd change this album. Here comes that duality part. The album needs the stinker songs, the ones that I personally dislike, because that's how it was made. I wouldn't change it for the world, since that's how it's always been. I don't know what the meaning is. I will still listen to the meaning. Even though I hate the meaning. It's still on that disc. It still is a part of the artwork. And for every track on this album I dislike, there still exists a part of me that still loves it.

So yeah, You bet I'll keep fighting this duality, and you bet I still love Supertramp. No matter how awful it can get. And trust me, Brother Where You Bound is so much worse than this album. I like to call that album "Rick Davies listens to one Pink Floyd album and says 'I could do that' and proceeds not to do that"
